Brands

Maison Éco

Maison Éco is a luxury brand committed to sustainability. They are known for using recycled fabrics and ensuring ethical labor practices, aiming to bring elegance without harming the environment.

Key Traits:
  • Luxury and sustainable fashion.
  • Use of recycled and organic materials.
  • Commitment to ethical labor practices.
Urban Vogue

Urban Vogue focuses on streetwear with a twist of high fashion. They represent the vibrant energy of the city through bold prints, comfortable fabrics, and a blend of casual and chic styles.

Key Traits:
  • Streetwear meets high fashion.
  • Bold prints and graphics.
  • Comfort combined with chic aesthetics.
Boho Luxe

Boho Luxe is a brand that embodies the bohemian spirit, focusing on flowing garments, intricate embroidery, and vibrant colors that promote a sense of freedom and creativity.

Key Traits:
  • Flowing, relaxed silhouettes.
  • Eclectic patterns and embroidery.
  • Vibrant color palette.
Uniqlo

Uniqlo is known for its affordable, high-quality basics that focus on functionality and simplicity. The brand emphasizes comfort, versatility, and innovative fabric technology.

Key Traits:
  • Minimalist, functional design.
  • High-quality basics and essentials.
  • Innovative fabric technology.
Zara

Zara is a leading fast-fashion brand known for its quick adaptation to current trends. Their styles are modern, trendy, and cater to a wide audience looking for up-to-date fashion.

Key Traits:
  • Fast-fashion with trendy designs.
  • Modern and accessible styles.
  • Focus on current, ever-changing trends.
Fear of God

Fear of God is a luxury streetwear brand blending high-end fashion with a laid-back, urban aesthetic. Known for its quality craftsmanship and unique approach, it appeals to those who appreciate casual luxury.

Key Traits:
  • Luxury streetwear with urban aesthetics.
  • Focus on quality craftsmanship.
  • Blend of casual style and high fashion.
