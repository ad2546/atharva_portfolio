Overview

The Finger Lakes region in upstate New York is a treasure trove of natural beauty and cultural experiences. It offers a peaceful escape by the water, wineries, and thrilling adventures in the great outdoors.

If relaxation is your goal, the Finger Lakes offers serene spa resorts and boutique accommodations with breathtaking views and farm-to-table meals.

History

The Finger Lakes has a history as rich as its landscapes, from the Haudenosaunee Confederacy to the Women’s Rights National Historical Park in Seneca Falls.

The region played a pivotal role in the abolitionist movement and the fight for women’s suffrage, making it a key historical destination.

Environment & Geology

The Finger Lakes owe their dramatic landscapes to glacial activity during the last ice age. Massive glaciers carved out the valleys, leaving behind steep gorges, towering waterfalls, and fertile soil that has become the lifeblood of the region’s agriculture.

The region’s geology also supports one of its most celebrated industries: winemaking. The fertile soil and unique microclimates have made the Finger Lakes a premier wine destination.

Current Events & Activities

The Finger Lakes region is a hub of year-round activity, offering something for everyone, no matter the season.

Foodies will rejoice in the Finger Lakes’ farm-to-table culinary scene, where chefs craft meals with ingredients sourced directly from local farms.
